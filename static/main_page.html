About the platform

Meta Consult is an AI-assisted business consultancy workflow. It helps organizations frame problems, surface root causes, and produce actionable recommendations using specialized agents, retrieval, and structured reporting—aligned to your industry and context.

Mission

We aim to make high-quality strategic support more accessible by pairing domain-style reasoning with repeatable process: consistent documentation, transparent stages, and outputs your stakeholders can review and execute.

How the workflow runs

Each engagement follows a clear sequence so quality stays predictable as complexity grows.

  1. Discovery — The consultant elicits scope, stakeholders, and success criteria through targeted questions.
  2. Proposal — A structured problem brief captures causes, impacts, and constraints across finance, operations, and strategy.
  3. Classification — A planner routes the work to the right domains (for example strategy, market, technology, or management).
  4. Research — Search-oriented agents gather external references, patterns, and benchmarks tied to those domains.
  5. Synthesis — The system merges conversation, analysis, and research into a cohesive solution narrative.
  6. Delivery — You receive a formatted PDF suitable for review, approval, and program kickoff.

How Meta Consult structures an engagement from intake to deliverable.

Meta Consult uses a staged pipeline: conversational intake, formalized problem analysis, domain routing, targeted research, and consolidated reporting. The sections below mirror what you will see as a session advances.

Stage 1

Initial consultation

You begin in the chat interface. The model asks focused questions about your organization, constraints, and desired outcomes. The goal is sufficient context before any automated document is produced.

Stage 2

Problem analysis and proposal

After enough turns (typically five or more exchanges), a proposal agent assembles a structured brief. That document usually covers:

  • Executive summary
  • Problem statement
  • Drivers and root causes
  • Financial, operational, and strategic impact

Stage 3

Domain classification

A planner reviews the proposal and assigns one or more domains so downstream research stays relevant. Examples include:

  • Strategic planning — growth, positioning, portfolio choices
  • Market research — customers, competition, demand signals
  • Technology — systems, automation, digital enablement
  • Management — operating model, processes, leadership cadence

Stage 4

Web search and research

For each active domain, search-oriented agents retrieve external material to ground recommendations. Typical sources of value include:

  • Documented practices and implementation patterns
  • Comparable situations and published outcomes
  • Current trends where timing matters to the recommendation
  • Tools or methods that map cleanly to your stated constraints

Stage 5

Solution generation

The system merges your narrative, the proposal, domain tags, and retrieved evidence into a single solution report. Expect:

  • An executive summary and prioritized actions
  • Phased implementation guidance
  • Risk and dependency discussion
  • Expected outcomes framed in business terms

Differentiators

  • Multi-agent design — separation of proposal, planning, search, and synthesis reduces single-model overload.
  • External grounding — research steps pull in material beyond static training data where appropriate.
  • Repeatable pipeline — the same stages apply to every engagement for predictable artifacts.
  • Account-specific context — profile fields and chat history inform the final narrative.
  • Observable progress — stage labels in the product reflect how far a session has moved.
